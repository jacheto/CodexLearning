At dawn the rooftops blush with gold,
And windows wake with warming fire;
The sparrows stitch the morning cold
With silver notes that climb still higher.

By noon the river learns to gleam,
Carrying clouds like folded letters;
Each stone remembers winter's dream,
Each willow writes in wind-soft fetters.

At dusk the alleys bloom in light,
Lanterns float where laughter lingers;
The moon unthreads the edge of night
And paints the dark with patient fingers.
